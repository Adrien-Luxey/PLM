Locate a Landing Zone

After 200 days of travelling space and eating cereals you finally reach Mars. Time to land at last!

The lander is on autopilot and all you have to do is look around and tell it where to land. The lander must land on a flat zone it you want to remain in one piece. You indicate the lander where to land by modifying the getLandingZone function so that it returns a Segment representing the zone of your choice.

A segment is composed of two Points, each of them composed of two doubles. A segment is built via the Segment(Point start, Point end) constructor. A point is built via the Point(double x, double y) constructor. A point's coordinates are accessible via its double x() and double y() methods.

In order to dertermine which zone to return, you'll need to call Iterable getGround(), which returns the vertices of the ground's profile from left to right.

One more thing: your function should return a valid flat zone in any kind of situation, not just for this particular one. That's why it will be not only be tested against the Challenging Terrain, Simple Configuration world, but also against the Simple Terrain, Simple Configuration one from the previous exercise.

Have a safe landing! Don't forget to put on your space suit on your way out.
